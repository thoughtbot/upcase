Design Critique

Each week you should be prepared to give and get critique on your work.

Checklist for getting critique:

  1. Have design brief ready.
  2. Have screenshot or link to site ready.
  3. Remember that this is meant to help. Its not meant as a personal attack.
  4. Interpret all critique as problems your design has not yet solved.
  5. Take notes on those problems and solve them after the critique is over.

Checklist for giving critique:

  1. Ask a lot questions.
  2. Think in terms of problems not solutions. Let the designer figure out the solutions.
  3. Review design brief and use to enforce your opinions.
  4. Keep in mind visual principles and use the design language that you are learning to enforce your opinions.

What should go into your design brief:

  1. A brief summary of the business / site goals. What problems are you trying to solve? What is the purpose of the app? (2-3 sentences)
  2. The key users. Describe behaviors, expectation, knowledge, or other characteristics. (One sentence each)
  3. Scenarios in which your app or site will be used. These should be short narratives of the above users with your app / site. (One sentence each)
  4. Design principles to follow. These are style guidelines for the qualities or characteristics you'd like the design to follow (2-3 words)
